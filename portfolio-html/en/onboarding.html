Why This Matters

The problem isn't information — it's delivery

Every company has the onboarding content. Almost none has a reliable way to surface it.

01 — The HR Burden

HR teams answer the same questions every single cohort

Verbal guidance doesn't scale, doesn't get recorded, and fails the moment a team member is unavailable. The new hire experience is inconsistent by default — and the cost accumulates silently every onboarding cycle.

02 — The AI Reliability Gap

Most chatbots fail silently — no one designs the fallback

When a model hallucinates or hits a confidence threshold, the new hire gets a confusing non-answer. Reliability in an HR context requires designed fallback logic, not just a capable model. That distinction is the product.

⚡

The real opportunity: replace the entire first-week information flow with a single, reliable, trackable system — while keeping humans in the loop exactly when it matters.

Demo Scenario

What a client sees in 5 minutes

A complete onboarding interaction — from first question to HR dashboard entry, showable live.

1
Day One Trigger

New hire opens app on their first day

No training required. The app opens to a guided welcome flow with checklist, FAQ cards, and a chat interface — all visible from one screen.

2
FAQ Route

"What documents do I need for payroll setup?"

Intent classifier recognizes a structured FAQ query. Routes directly to a pre-built FAQ card — no AI generation, instant and reliable.

3
Checklist Trigger

Completion prompt activates guided checklist

After the FAQ response, the app prompts the user to complete the payroll setup checklist. Structured task flow with progress tracking — no chat needed.

4
AI Chat Route

"What's the vacation policy during probation?"

Edge case question routes to AI chatbot. OpenAI API generates a contextual answer. Model confidence is within threshold — response delivered directly.

5
Fallback Logic

Low-confidence query triggers safe fallback

Unusual question hits the confidence threshold. Fallback handler surfaces a canned safe response and flags the question for HR review — no silent failure.

6
HR Ops Visibility

HR dashboard: 4 questions, 2 checklist items, 0 gaps

The client's screen shows the new hire's session: questions asked, checklist completion status, and any fallback flags. One onboarding session just produced structured ops data.
